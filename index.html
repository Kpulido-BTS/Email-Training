📧 Client Email Etiquette Training

This training outlines expectations for professional, client‑facing email communication. All agents are required to review this material before completing the knowledge check.

Basic Email Etiquette for Client Communication

1. Use a Professional Email Address

Always use your official company email account for client communication.

2. Write a Clear, Relevant Subject Line

Subject lines should clearly reflect the purpose of the email.

Example: Project Update – Q2 Marketing Campaign

3. Greet the Client Properly

Use the client’s correct name and title unless a first‑name basis is established.

4. Keep the Tone Professional and Courteous

Avoid slang, emojis, or overly casual language.

5. Be Concise but Complete

Include all necessary details so the client does not need to follow up.

6. Structure for Readability

Use short paragraphs and bullet points when appropriate.

7. Proofread Before Sending

Review grammar, spelling, formatting, and accuracy.

8. Respond Promptly

Acknowledge client emails within 24 hours.

9. Be Mindful of Attachments

Clearly label attachments and reference them in the email body.

10. Close Professionally

Please let me know if you have any questions.

Using Front Features to Support Communication

Fix Grammar

The Fix Grammar feature may be used to help correct grammar and spelling. Employees must review the message before sending.

Adjust Tone

The Adjust Tone feature allows messages to be revised as Professional, Friendly, or Empathetic. Employees remain responsible for the final content.
